Abstract

Keystroke dynamics is a behavioral biometric that uses an individual's typing rhythm to distinguish real users from impostors on standard keyboards. Traditional authentication methods that depend only on passwords or one-time passcodes are at risk of theft, reuse, and phishing attacks. This system introduces a rhythm-based identity verification approach that models users' typing patterns using timing features such as key hold time and inter-key latency, applying supervised machine-learning techniques for decision making.
